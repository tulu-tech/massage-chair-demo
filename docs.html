01 What Is This System?

This is a digital sales assistant for a massage chair showroom. When a customer walks in, a sales rep opens this app on a tablet. The app guides the conversation, learns what the customer needs, and recommends the best chair — all while collecting data that helps with follow-up after the visit.

🎯 For the Customer

It feels like a premium consultation — not a boring survey. They answer simple questions about their lifestyle, and the system finds their perfect chair match.

🧑‍💼 For the Sales Rep

It gives them a script, tells them which chairs to show, what to emphasize during the demo, and what objections to expect. After the visit, it writes the follow-up messages for them.

📊 For the Owner

It creates a CRM dashboard showing every customer, their interest level (hot/warm/cold), which chair they liked, and exactly what to do next to close the sale.

The Big Idea: Half the real buying decision happens during and after the physical demo — not during the initial questions. This system captures both halves and combines them into one smart recommendation.

The 2 Bonus Questions

After question 10, the system looks at all the answers and finds the customer's biggest concern (we call it their "friction"). Then it asks 2 more questions about that specific concern. There are 6 possible concern categories:

🤝 Partner/Spouse Decision

"Would a side-by-side summary help you review this together?" and "What would matter most to your partner?"

💰 Payment Comfort

"Do you prefer lowest monthly payment or best long-term value?" and "Would cash vs. financing comparison help?"

📐 Space/Fit Concerns

"Where will the chair go?" and "Do you know the room measurements?"

🔍 Comparison Overload

"Would you rather see the best 2 or compare 3–4?" and "What would simplify the decision most?"

🛡️ Confidence/Trust

"What would help you feel fully confident?" and "Is this your first serious massage chair purchase?"

🤔 "Let Me Think About It"

"What would help you feel ready sooner?" and "If everything checked out, when would feel right?"

07 The Guided Demo Screen

After the customer sees their chair recommendation, the rep presses "Begin Showroom Demo." A strategy screen appears that tells the rep exactly how to run the physical demo. This screen is for the rep's eyes only.

What the Rep Sees

✅ What to Emphasize

A list of features to highlight during the demo. For example: "deep-tissue intensity, neck/shoulder precision, lower-body recovery, guided programs." These are chosen based on the customer's body focus and lifestyle answers.

🚫 What NOT to Lead With

Things to avoid mentioning first. For example: "Don't lead with aesthetics over function" or "Don't show more than 2 chairs initially." This prevents the rep from overwhelming the customer.

⚠️ Likely Objection

The most probable objection the customer will raise. For example: "Let me think about it" or "I need to check with my spouse." The rep can mentally prepare a response before the customer even says it.

💡 Why These Two

A short explanation of why the #1 and backup chairs were chosen. For example: "Focus on active recovery feel and body-specific attention. Lead with the strongest deep-tissue models in their budget."

09 How the Final Decision Is Made

After the rep submits the post-demo report, the system combines everything it knows — both the question answers (discovery) and the demo observations — into one final intelligence report.

The Weight Split: Discovery 35% + Demo 65%

The system believes that what happens during the physical demo matters more than what the customer said before trying the chairs. So demo signals count for almost twice as much.

📝 Discovery Weight (35%)

The lead heat score from the 12 questions. A customer who said "ASAP," "pain/tension," and "nothing stops me" would have a high discovery score (~85/100). A customer who said "just researching," "just exploring," and "let me think about it" would score low (~25/100).

🪑 Demo Weight (65%)

Calculated from the rep's 28-field report. Points are awarded for engagement (+12), positive reactions (+15), decisiveness (+15), buying signals (+6 each), and next-step commitment (+20). Points are subtracted for strong objections (-10).

Lead Temperature

The combined score (0–100) determines the lead's "temperature":

11 Product Knowledge Database

The system has deep knowledge about every single chair — not just the name and price. For each of the 15 chairs, the database stores detailed information that powers the matching algorithm and the customer-facing recommendation screen.

What's Stored For Each Chair

💰 Pricing

Original list price, current sale price, financing availability (Affirm), and extended warranty option.

🎨 Availability

In-stock status, estimated delivery time (5–14 business days), and available color options.

🎯 Positioning

Who this chair is best for (primary use case), secondary use cases, and positioning notes for the rep.

💆 Massage System

Type of mechanism (3D, 4D, dual), massage actions (kneading, tapping, shiatsu), and roller design details.

🔥 Heat System

Which body zones have heat, temperature type (infrared, ceramic), and coverage area.

♻️ Zero Gravity

Number of recline stages, whether it has True Zero Gravity, and the recline angle.

💨 Air Massage

Which body zones have air compression (shoulders, arms, hips, calves, feet).

📏 Dimensions

Upright and reclined footprint, wall clearance needed, and weight capacity.

Why this matters: When the Match Screen shows a recommendation, the description, price, and color options you see are pulled directly from this database — not hardcoded. If a price changes, updating it in one place updates it everywhere in the app.
